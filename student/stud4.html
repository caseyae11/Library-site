Story 1 (student)

Sam responds to confirm that the photo of individuals in wheelchairs is copyrighted and shouldn’t be used in Afshin’s online project without permission. Since the image is used for a decorative purpose in their project, it is likely not fair dealing under the copyright law. For an image to fall under fair dealing, it must be used for research, education, private study, parody or satire, criticism, review or news reporting. While citing the image is necessary for academic integrity, it is not the same as copyright compliance. In this case, Afshin should contact the photographer for permission.

Sam explains what Afshin needs to do to request permission. Afshin emails the person who posted the image to ask if they are the copyright owner and if they are, whether they would grant permission to use it.
