able to find OER on other topics

Professor Zhou shares the OER she found with her colleagues and Associate Dean at a meeting. The group is excited about these resources, since students can save some money by not having to buy as many textbooks. Her colleagues are intrigued to hear that they can adapt and remix the content in these OER.
They have a few copyright questions related to how they can use and adapt OER. They also want to continue to use some of the chapters from their current textbooks.

Professor Zhou emails Sam Cheng, Open Education and Copyright Librarian, and shares her colleagues' questions. Sam explains what the Creative Commons licenses* on each OER mean in terms of being able to copy and adapt the content. Sam suggests using a tool called Pressbooks to adapt and remix the content and explains how they can get free accounts through eCampusOntario to access it.

Sam offers to provide additional support to the professor and her colleagues as needed. She also refers Professor Zhou to reach out to SPARK for help on incorporating OER into their instructional materials and digital learning objects.
